Established in 1966, HKTDC has grown alongside Hong Kong itself. From championing local exports during the city’s manufacturing boom, to positioning Hong Kong as a world‑class hub for trade fairs and services, and later as a two‑way gateway connecting global markets with the Chinese Mainland, the HKTDC has continually evolved with the times. Over the past decade, it has harnessed major national initiatives such as the Belt and Road Initiative and the Greater Bay Area to strengthen Hong Kong’s role as an international connector. Sixty years on, the HKTDC continues to drive opportunity, innovation and growth

Power Partner
 

 
 
The British Film Institute (BFI) is the UK’s lead organisation for film and the moving image. Founded in 1933, it is a cultural charity and National Lottery distributor that supports screen creativity, preserves the BFI National Archive, and works with industry and government to strengthen the UK’s screen sector.
 
At the 30th Hong Kong International Film & TV Market (FILMART), held from 17–20 March, we worked closely with the BFI as the institutional partner of the Producers’ Connect Programme. Five UK producers — Alex Gordon, Camilla Gatin, Johnny Schumann, Lee Thomas and Sophie Vickers — took part in fireside chats, business matching, location tours and Hong Kong Night, fostering meaningful international industry connections.

HKpedia
 
Hong Kong Sevens is celebrating its 50th anniversary in 2026, marking half a century since the first tournament kicked off in 1976! Over the years, it has grown into one of the world’s most iconic rugby sevens events. In 2025, the tournament brought together around 30 top international teams and over 40,000 spectators, including Hong Kong’s own national side and fans from across the globe. But Hong Kong Sevens is more than just rugby. It showcases Hong Kong’s unique mix of sport, culture, and excitement, attracting fans from around the world.
 
The 2026 edition will take place from 17 to 19 April at Kai Tak Sports Park. Why not plan a trip to Hong Kong during this period and experience the action, the passion, and the electric atmosphere of one of rugby’s greatest tournaments firsthand?
